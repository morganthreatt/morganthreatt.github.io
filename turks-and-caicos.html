Introduction

Turks and Caicos Islands were discovered first in 1512 by europeans. Since then the islands have always been claimed by european powers and eventually the Brittish empire took control of the islands, that is where the history begins.

Turks and Caicos Islands were apart of the United Kingdoms's Jamaican colony for a while, until Jamaica assumed it's own status as an independent colony.

The Islands were also associated with the Bahamas. The governor of the Bahamas ran Turks and Caicos Islands and their affairs for eight years from 1965 until 1973. When the Bahamas became indepent, Turks and Caicos Islands then had to recieve new leadership in 1973, a completely new governor.

Turks and Caicos islands became indepent in 1982, however since polocies have changed since then the Islands are still considered Brittish overseen territory.

In 2009 the UK took away the power Turks and Caicos Islands had to self govern due to allegations of corruption. In 2012 power was restored, still to be overseen by the United Kingdom.

Geography

Location

Turks and Caicos Islands are two island groups in the North Atlantic Ocean, southeast of the Bahamas and north of Haiti. A lot of people assume Turks and Caicos Islands are apart of the Carribean, but that is not tecnically true.

Climate

The climate on the islands is mostly tropical, there is a lot of marine life and it is almost always sunny, however, the climate at times can also be described as being dry as well.

Communication

Telecommunication System

The Turks and Caicos Islands are completely digital, they also offer international dialing as well. There is a full range of services when it comes to technology and residents are able to access the internt normally as we would in the United States.

Unfortunately wih the outbreak of COVID-19, spending has been cut on these services in many households in Turks and Caicos.

Broadcast Media

There are no local TV stations on the islands. People are able to recieve broadcasts from the Bahamas, there are about 15 local stations there. Of course with purchase there are more channels to choose from.

Internet Country Code

The internet country code for Turks and Caicos Islands is .tc
